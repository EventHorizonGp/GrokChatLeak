Executive Summary: PQShield

PQShield is a private, for-profit company founded in 2018 and headquartered in Oxford, UK.
Founders

Dr. Ali El Kaafarani is the founder, CEO, and a leading post-quantum cryptography expert originally from Lebanon. He holds a PhD in cryptography from the University of Bath and conducted postdoctoral research at the University of Oxford's Mathematical Institute. Professionally, he was a research fellow at Oxford, focusing on cryptographic protocols, and has contributed extensively to international standards. His key achievements include co-authoring multiple NIST post-quantum cryptography (PQC) standards, publishing over 50 academic papers on cryptography, and founding PQShield to commercialize quantum-resistant solutions. The CTO is Dr. Thomas Prest, with a background in lattice-based cryptography from ENS Paris and IBM Research; his achievements include breakthroughs in efficient PQC implementations and contributions to NIST finalist algorithms like Falcon.
pqshield.com
Product

PQShield develops quantum-resistant cryptography solutions, including hardware IP cores and software libraries designed to protect against quantum computing threats. The unique innovation lies in its end-to-end PQC suite, which integrates NIST-standardized algorithms like ML-KEM, ML-DSA, and SLH-DSA into chips, applications, and cloud environments, making it the first to deliver quantum-safe crypto on hardware. Products include PQMicroLib-Core (ultra-small library for embedded systems), PQPerform series (high-performance hardware accelerators), PQCryptoLib-SDK (plug-and-play for OpenSSL), and PQPlatform-CoPro (secure coprocessor for integration). These enable customers to upgrade security seamlessly, reducing vulnerability to "harvest now, decrypt later" attacks, ensuring compliance with emerging standards, and minimizing performance overhead‚Äîoften achieving sub-1% impact on efficiency‚Äîwhile lowering migration costs through hybrid classical-PQC support.
