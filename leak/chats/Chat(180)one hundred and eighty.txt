Anthony Perkins is a Silicon Valley trailblazer, a serial entrepreneur, and media innovator renowned for his knack for connecting tech entrepreneurs with investors and shaping the conversation around technology trends. He’s a male figure (so we’ll use "he" and "his") who has left a significant mark on the tech and venture capital world by founding or co-founding several influential organizations. Here’s who he is, what he’s known for, and his standout professional accomplishments tied to SVB, Churchill Club, Red Herring, AlwaysOn, and Cryptonite.
Who He Is and What He’s Known For

Anthony Perkins has built a career creating platforms that bridge the gap between tech innovators and the resources they need to succeed. He’s a visionary with a track record of spotting emerging trends—like the Internet boom and the rise of Web3—while fostering communities that drive tech progress. His work spans banking, media, events, and now decentralized networks, making him a key player in Silicon Valley’s evolution.

Here’s a rundown of his roles with the organizations you mentioned:

    Silicon Valley Bank (SVB): Perkins co-founded the Technology Group at SVB, helping transform it into a powerhouse bank with $200 billion in assets, serving tech entrepreneurs globally.
    Churchill Club: He launched this non-profit speaker’s forum, growing it to 10,000 members and hosting tech giants and world leaders like Presidents Bill Clinton and George W. Bush.
    Red Herring: Perkins founded this magazine in the 1990s, turning it into a must-read for Internet boom coverage, with a peak subscriber base of over 600,000.
    AlwaysOn: As founder and editor, he created a global events and blog brand that hosted entrepreneurial summits and spotlighted top private companies through its influential "AlwaysOn Global Top 250 Private Company" list.
    Cryptonite: Currently, he’s the founder and editor of Cryptonite, a platform aiming to connect entrepreneurs and investors in the Web3 space via a decentralized social network where creators own their content and earn crypto rewards.

He’s widely recognized for his ability to anticipate tech shifts—most famously predicting the dot-com bust in his book The Internet Bubble—and for building ecosystems that empower innovation.
His Biggest Professional Accomplishments

Perkins’ career is packed with wins, but here are his most significant achievements tied to these organizations:

    Pioneering Tech Banking with SVB
        Co-founding the Technology Group at Silicon Valley Bank was a game-changer. Perkins helped SVB become a go-to financial partner for tech startups, supporting countless entrepreneurs as the bank grew into a $200 billion asset institution.

        Founding the Churchill Club gave tech leaders a stage to share ideas. Hosting nearly every major tech executive and global figures like U.S. presidents, it’s become a cornerstone of Silicon Valley’s intellectual ecosystem.
    Chronicling the Internet Boom with Red Herring
        Launching Red Herring put Perkins at the forefront of the 1990s Internet revolution. The magazine’s in-depth coverage reached over 600,000 subscribers, cementing its influence during a pivotal tech era.

        Through AlwaysOn, Perkins produced high-profile summits and curated the "AlwaysOn Global Top 250 Private Company" list, identifying breakout stars like YouTube (featured just months after its founding). Over 50% of the 1,483 companies listed across its run achieved successful exits via acquisitions or IPOs.
    Predicting the Dot-Com Bust
        Co-authoring The Internet Bubble showcased Perkins’ foresight. Published before the 2000 crash, it accurately warned of the dot-com bubble’s collapse, earning him credibility as a tech prophet.
    Leading the Web3 Charge with Cryptonite
        Now, Perkins is pioneering Cryptonite, a decentralized social network for Web3 entrepreneurs and investors. He sees Web3 as a $100 trillion wealth opportunity, and Cryptonite aims to empower creators with ownership and crypto-based rewards—a bold step into the future.

Why It Matters

Anthony Perkins isn’t just a founder—he’s a connector and a trendsetter. His platforms have shaped how tech entrepreneurs access capital, share ideas, and build wealth, from the Internet age to the blockchain era.
